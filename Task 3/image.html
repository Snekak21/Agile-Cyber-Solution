The Lotus is an aquatic plant that is native to Southeast and East Asia. The scientific name of the lotus is Nelumbo Nucifera. It is also referred to as Indian lotus or sacred lotus. Lotus plants grow in stationery water bodies like ponds and lakes. They also grow in muddy, slow-moving rivers and delta areas. Lotus flowers are usually white or light pink in colour and look very beautiful.

Lotus leaves are large and circular. This flower is considered sacred in Hinduism as well as Buddhism and has a lot of religious, cultural and historical significance. It represents purity, knowledge and spiritual awakening. Lotus is the national flower of India.

Lotus blossoms are very beautiful and have a lot of petals. Some Chinese lotuses can have around 3000 petals in one flower while a Japanese variety known as “the strange lotus” can have up to 5000 petals.
